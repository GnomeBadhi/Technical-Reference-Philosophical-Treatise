CLAIM 1  
A system for determining value transformation comprising:  
(a) a set of primitives representing tangible work, attention influence, and agreement;  
(b) a set of invariants constraining the conditions under which value may be created or altered; and  
(c) a computational mechanism for deriving an influence measure from said primitives.

CLAIM 2  
The system of Claim 1 wherein the tangible work primitive is conserved across the system boundary.

CLAIM 3  
The system of Claim 1 wherein the attention influence primitive is non‑conserved and contributes to the influence measure through a monotonic mapping.

CLAIM 4  
The system of Claim 1 wherein the agreement primitive is non‑conserved and contributes to the influence measure through a weighted term.

CLAIM 5  
The system of Claim 1 wherein the invariants comprise:  
(a) a requirement for tangible linkage;  
(b) a requirement for attention;  
(c) a requirement for agreement; and  
(d) a requirement for auditability and non‑interruptibility.

CLAIM 6  
The system of Claim 1 wherein the influence measure is computed as a weighted combination of the primitives.

CLAIM 7  
The system of Claim 1 wherein a value delta is computed as an aggregation of influence measures.

CLAIM 8  
A method for determining value transformation comprising:  
(a) receiving the primitives;  
(b) applying the invariants;  
(c) computing the influence measure; and  
(d) computing the value delta.

CLAIM 9  
The method of Claim 8 wherein the invariants restrict transformations that violate boundary conditions or axioms.
